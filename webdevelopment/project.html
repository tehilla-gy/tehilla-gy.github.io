PROJECT MANAGEMENT INTERNSHIP

Over the summers of 2022 and 2023 I worked with Gilmor Engineering limited, a contractor working for the Federal Capital Development Authority in Abuja, the capital city of Nigeria. During my project management internship, I worked closely with engineers, contractors, and supervisors on various stages of highway construction projects. I gained hands-on experience in scheduling, budgeting, and on-site coordination. My responsibilities included reviewing project plans, assisting in tracking progress, and attending coordination meetings to ensure timely and cost-effective execution. I also contributed to quality control efforts by conducting field inspections and verifying that construction activities met design specifications and safety standards. This role gave me valuable exposure to the fast-paced and collaborative environment of construction management. I learned how to navigate challenges such as weather delays, subcontractor coordination, and material delivery timelines, all while maintaining effective communication across different stakeholders. ENGINEERING PEER MENTOR

As an engineering peer mentor, I supported first- and second-year engineering students by helping them transition into the academic and social environment of the engineering program. I served as a resource for course planning, study strategies, and navigating university life, drawing from my own experiences to provide guidance and encouragement. Through regular one-on-one check-ins and group mentoring sessions, I fostered a supportive space where students could ask questions, share concerns, and build confidence. I also collaborated with faculty and staff to organize academic workshops and community-building events, aiming to strengthen retention and engagement among underrepresented students in STEM. This role deepened my communication and leadership skills while reinforcing my own understanding of core engineering concepts. It was incredibly rewarding to witness the growth of my mentees and know that I played a part in their academic journeys. Mentoring taught me the value of empathy, patience, and the impact of peer support in engineering education.

ASCE KIDS DAY

I volunteered at the ASCE Kids Day event hosted by my school's chapter, which introduced 3rd to 6th grade students to various branches of civil engineering through fun, hands-on activities. The event featured rotating stations focused on structural, transportation, environmental, and geotechnical engineering. I was stationed in the transportation section, where I helped guide students through an interactive activity designed to teach them about traffic flow, road safety, and basic transportation planning concepts. We used miniature road layouts and toy cars to simulate different traffic scenarios, sparking conversations about signal timing, pedestrian safety, and congestion. It was rewarding to see the kids so curious and excited about engineering, and to help them connect real-world problems to simple solutions. Volunteering at this event strengthened my communication skills and reminded me how important outreach is for inspiring future engineers. It also reinforced my own passion for transportation engineering and STEM education.
